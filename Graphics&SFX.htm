GRAPHICS AND SFX

Visuals

I can't honestly say that this game has the best graphics ever (the battle sequences from Final Fantasy are probably the greatest in-game graphics known to man, and lets not forget those crazy water effects in Wave Race 64) but in terms of sheer frequency/regularity, the graphics are unmatched on the N64, and few games on other platforms can even begin to touch this game's visuals. Its one thing to have beautiful rendered backgrounds (like in Final Fantasy 8), but this is an entirely 3D world and there is NO pop- up/draw-in whatsoever. This may not sound like much, but there is something incredibly satisfying when you can see far off into the distance in any given environment. It helps solidify that feeling of completeness, and gives added depth of realism. After visiting some of the game's locales, and seeing no graphical glitches or pop-up, you begin to see these environments as real places that you'd like to return to, and not just texture-mapped polygons. Among my favorite locales: the cemetery, Lake Hylia and Hyrule Fields. To be riding aimlessly around Hyrule Field on horseback, wading through the clear blue waters or standing in the pouring rain, soaking in environmental details... is nothing short of a religious experience for me. I love the look and feel of the game's many dungeons and certain boss encounters are like battling with Call spells from Final Fantasy 7. So, although I can't honestly say this game has the best graphics ever, for me the visuals do exactly what they're meant to do: create a realistic fantasy setting... and that means I couldn't ask for anything more. Of course, all environments will have some sort of wall to prevent you from exiting, but the locales are so huge chances are you won't notice them much and hey, they never bothered me much.

Atmospheric Details Galore

Z64 comes packed with atmospheric details. For example, when you enter certain areas (or play the Song of Storms on your ocarina) everything gets dark and the sky fills with dark clouds and it starts raining! You'll see flashes of lightning in the sky, amidst the rain falling all around you! The graveyard is easily one of the most atmospheric gaming environments ever made. The following are some of my favorite details:

-Link has a shadow, and depending on the light sources in the environment (the sun, torches or even the light emanating from his fairy Navi) actually affects the shadow. It grows long or shortens, changes direction and fades depending on Link's proximity to the light, and it looks awesome.
-Depending on the temperature of the environment you're in, Link will react to the heat/cold in his idle animations. Leave the controller alone for a moment while adventuring and Link will sigh and wipe sweat from his forehead, or shiver and sneeze!
-I love the animation routines for when Link mounts/dismounts his horse, or how he gently pats Epona's neck in the idle animation.
-Graphically, the game does some interesting lens effects for certain environments. For example, when in extremely hot locations or when you first dive underwater, the lens of the camera will actually distort and fluctuate!
-If you dive underwater, Link eventually comes up for air with a great *AH!* as he gasps for air.
-If you fall/jump from a great height, and don't roll out of the impact, Link will land with a *yelp* and shudder with pain as the impact racks his body.
-Sometimes it gets foggy, but the fog is used only for atmospheric flavor (not to cover pop-up).
-Link will gently sway with the music he creates using his ocarina.
-The physics when Link wears the iron boots underwater, or when child Link dons the bunny ears are also fantastic.
-Of special note is the way the mirror shield reflects light. Standing under a focused beam of light allows Link to take out the mirror shield and control the direction in which the light hits the walls, ceiling, and floor (it looks really great!). The mirror shield will also reflect light sources around Link, such as the blue light emanating from warp points generated after a boss encounter!
-Typical of most N64 games, there are certain objects and enemies which use a reflection map to look shiny. This is an effect that has been used rarely on other consoles, if at all (Xenogears has used it correctly once, and Wipeout XL uses a fake reflection map for medals obtained).
-Sparks, shockwaves and simulated particle effects flash across the screen when your sword or other items make contact with walls, enemy shields or even just rocky/hard terrain. These are just some of the cool visuals you'll come across in the game... Its totally awesome!

 The Music

So many N64 games have pretty crappy music. Even some of the better games out there just don't sound all that great. But Zelda's music is actually quite good. You'll remember some of the themes from past games, such as Ganon's theme. But there are plenty of great new songs too. From the haunting themes of the dungeons you'll have to explore, to the upbeat tunes of Kakariko village, you won't have any complaints. And the music does some interesting transitions, which are usually unnoticeable. The ocarina melodies you can play are also ingenious little songs in themselves, considering their simplicity. There's an orchestrated version of Z64's soundtrack, which may be imported, and believe me its GREAT! Although most songs seem to be played by a quartet of stringed instruments, there are some songs taught have a full orchestra. Just about anyone who's been playing games since the good old NES days should LOVE hearing the classic Zelda theme song, Ganon's theme or a combination of the various themes from all the Zelda games ever made (excluding of course those nasty, nasty CD-I games). Besides that, the CD also includes stirring renditions of Z64's locale themes, like the Gerudo Desert (which sounds 100 times better orchestrated), as well as an unsynthed version of Malon's singing, a song containing all the ocarina melodies, and more! Its a great album I think any Zelda fan (even those who don't even own the N64 like myself!) should get. I'm going to try and put up AT LEAST one MP3 file so you can hear an orchestrated song or two.

Ambient Sound Effects Galore

Z64 comes packed with ambient sound effects galore! Of course, not *all* the SFX need to be ambient, and the ones that aren't are what you'd expect: sword slashes, hookshot hits, the cloppity-clop of your horse, etc. its all good. But the ambient sounds are really something special. For example, when it begins to rain, you can hear harsh thunder booming overhead, and the actual sound of the rain drops is perfect! If you turn the volume way up, close your eyes... its like actually standing outside in the rain! Certain areas get windy, and sometimes your actions echo if you're in a cavern with the proper acoustics. To signal the change from night to day, you'll hear a rooster's cockadoodledo, and when its night you'll hear a wolf baying at the moon somewhere (if you're under water, these noises get all warped). At night time it generally gets pretty quiet, so you can hear all sorts of creatures lurking somewhere - but its not meant to be scary or anything. The SFX are so good you'll know when there's a bat in the room, and you can even use them to locate those damned gold skulltulas! Its just really great detail. Other SFX that are spot-on include: Link's foot steps, running water (waterfalls), flames, and Link's various vocalizations. And, lest we forget, you'll enjoy hearing that classic Zelda "dee- do-do-do-dee" when you uncover a secret or solve a maddening puzzle - its nostalgia on the same level as hearing that classic Final Fantasy theme song with the harps!
